RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN ARTS AND HUMANITIES

Art and Humanities have a harmonious relationship.The humanities look at and interpret art, whereas the humanities deliver perspectives and ideas that enrich our appreciation of art. They communicate constantly with one another.



ART as

  • EXPRESSION

    • The humanities encourage us to comprehend appreciating the historical, cultural, and social settings that create creative expression. Understanding the social commentary in a protest song, for example, broadens our knowledge of its artistic integrity.


  • INTERPRETATION

    • Art can be applied when evaluating historical events, abstract ideas, and cultural subtleties. A rendition of a historical battle, for example, might provide a distinct viewpoint on the war.



HUMANITIES as

  • INSPIRATION

    • Artists establish inspiration from historical events, philosophical thoughts, and literary works, which deepens their creative process and brings greater meaning to their work.


  • COMMUNICATION

    • Vast ideas and emotions may be communicated through art in ways that words alone cannot. The humanities give skills for understanding these subtle meanings and the artist's purpose.
